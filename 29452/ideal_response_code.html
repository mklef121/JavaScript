Nigerian Agriculture Sector Analysis

Key Historical Data

Nigeria's agricultural sector's contribution to GDP has been relatively stable, ranging from 24.45% in 2016 to 25.70% in 2020. Despite the oil sector's dominance, agriculture remains crucial, employing over one-third of the population.

  • 2016

    Contribution to GDP: 24.45%

  • 2020

    Contribution to GDP: 25.70%

Production vs. Demand

Production levels for key crops are falling short of national demands. For example, rice production was 86.5 million metric tons in 2020 against a demand of 117 million metric tons, while maize production fell to 18 million metric tons from a demand of 23 million metric tons.

Fisheries and Aquaculture

Fisheries and aquaculture play a significant role in Nigeria's food supply and employment. Recent investments in aquaculture have led to increased fish production, yet there are still issues related to overfishing and habitat degradation that need to be addressed.
